# 🔴 Push Workout Guide (Chest, Shoulders & Triceps)

This document outlines the optimal push-day training strategy, targeting the chest (pectorals), anterior and lateral shoulders (deltoids), and triceps. It focuses on biomechanically sound exercises, progressive overload, and stretch-focused training.

---

## 1. Cornerstone Movements & Exercise Swaps

To build a powerful and healthy upper body, prioritize movements that maximize muscular tension while protecting joints and rotator cuffs.

### Upper Body Push Cornerstones
1. **Vertical Push**: **Seated Dumbbell Overhead Press**
   * *Primary Muscles*: Anterior deltoid, lateral deltoid, upper chest.
   * *Rationale*: Seated pressing is superior to standing because it reduces stability demands. This allows heavier loading and yields roughly **10% greater strength** output.
2. **Horizontal Push**: **Flat Barbell Bench Press** (or Dumbbell Bench Press)
   * *Primary Muscles*: Pectorals (chest), anterior deltoids, triceps.
   * *Rationale*: There is a direct, strong correlation between bench press strength and chest hypertrophy. Use dumbbells if barbells cause shoulder discomfort, as dumbbells offer greater range of motion and joint comfort.

### Biomechanical Push Swaps

Swap out common, structurally limiting exercises for variations that load muscles in their stretched positions:

| Less Optimal Exercise | Biomechanical Limitation | Superior Alternative | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Push-Up** | Limited chest stretch at the bottom due to the floor; difficult to progress. | **Elevated Push-Up (Deficit)** | Use parallettes or blocks to sink below hand level, creating a deep chest stretch. |
| **Dumbbell Tricep Kickback** | Zero tension in the stretched position; only hard at the very top. | **Overhead Cable Extension** | Maximizes passive stretch on the triceps' long head under constant cable tension. |
| **Incline Push-Up** | Standard body weight variation with limited range of motion. | **Incline DB Press / Deficit Push-Up** | Better load auto-regulation and deeper mechanical stretch for the upper chest. |

## 2. Chest Training (Lower Pecs Focus)

To build fully rounded lower pectorals, focus on exercises that mimic the downward sweep of the lower chest fibers. Decline pressing is unnecessary; flat pressing and extension/crossover movements are superior.

> [!TIP]
> Decline pressing is not required for lower chest development. Understanding chest anatomy and function allows you to isolate the lower pecs using flat angles and high-to-low sweeps.

### Suggested Chest Exercises

#### 1. Flat Bench Press (Barbell or Dumbbell)
* **Setup**: Retract and depress your shoulder blades, maintain a slight natural arch in your lower back, and plant feet firmly.
* **Execution**: Use a medium-to-wide grip. Lower the bar to the mid-chest with control, pause briefly at the bottom stretch, and press up.
* **Volume**: 3–4 sets of 6–8 reps.

#### 2. Bench Pullovers
* **Setup**: Lie perpendicular across a flat bench, placing only your upper back on the bench. Lower your hips below bench level to increase the stretch.
* **Execution**: Hold a dumbbell overhead. Lower it behind your head with a slight elbow bend, focusing entirely on the stretch. Pull it back up to just above your forehead (do not bring it over the chest, which removes tension).
* **Volume**: 3 sets of 10–12 reps.

#### 3. High-to-Low Cable Crossovers
* **Setup**: Set cable pulleys to the highest position. Take a staggered stance for stability and keep your torso upright.
* **Execution**: Pull the handles down and together to meet at waist level. Focus on a downward sweep.
* **Advanced Variation**: Cross your hands slightly at the bottom to maximize lower pectoral contraction.
* **Volume**: 3 sets of 12–15 reps.

### Suggested Chest Day Structure

| Exercise | Sets | Reps | Focus |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :--- |
| **Flat Bench Press** | 3–4 | 6–8 | Primary compound movement for overall mass. |
| **Bench Pullovers** | 3 | 10–12 | Targets lower chest through shoulder extension stretch. |
| **High-to-Low Cable Crossovers** | 3 | 12–15 | Finisher with high tension at the bottom contraction. |

## 3. Shoulder Pressing & Lateral Raises (Front & Side Delts)

The deltoid is highly segmented. To build wide, "boulder shoulders," prioritize the lateral (side) heads. The front (anterior) delts receive substantial stimulation during chest presses.

> [!WARNING]
> Avoid performing hypermobility exercises like passing a PVC pipe behind the back during shoulder warm-ups. This stresses the labrum and rotator cuff. Maintain proper scapular movement and avoid overstretching.

### Shoulder Warm-up: Mobility
* **Focus**: Emphasize scapulohumeral rhythm. Avoid excessive range of motion to prevent impingement. Warm up the rotator cuffs with light external rotations.

### Shoulder Exercises

#### 1. Cable Side Lateral
* **Execution**: Set the cable pulley to wrist height. Stand next to it, and raise the cable out sideways. Focus on **internally rotating** the shoulder (pinky finger slightly higher than thumb) to target neglected fibers in the lateral delt.
* **Execution Tip**: Avoid lifting past parallel (shoulder height) to prevent the trapezius from taking over.
* **Hypertrophy Boost**: Focus on pulling out rather than up.

#### 2. Smith Machine Wide Grip Upright Row
* **Execution**: Grip the bar wide. Pull it up along the chest, keeping elbows high above the bar to contract the side delts. Focus on internal shoulder rotation.

#### 3. Anterior Delts Press (Tucked-Elbow Shoulder Press)
* **Execution**: Perform seated dumbbell presses with your elbows tucked slightly forward (in the scapular plane, ~45 degrees) rather than flared wide. This isolates the front delt and protects the shoulder joint.

#### 4. Makeshift Viking Press
* **Execution**: Use a barbell landmine setup. Take a neutral grip and press upward. This is an excellent, joint-friendly pressing movement to finish off the anterior delts.

## 4. Triceps Training (三頭肌訓練)

Triceps training should target the long head (requires overhead stretch), lateral head, and medial head. 

* **Frequency (頻率)**: 1–2 times per week, with at least 48 hours of recovery between sessions. (每週1至2次，間隔至少48小時恢復。)
* **Rest Between Sets (每組間休息)**: 60–90 seconds. (60–90秒。)
* **Intensity (強度)**: Push the last set of each exercise to failure, then continue with lengthened partial reps (half-reps at the stretch). (最後一組推至力竭後，繼續進行延伸的部分反覆動作。)

### Triceps Workout Structure (三頭肌訓練結構)

#### 1. Overhead Cable Extension (上拉繩三頭肌伸展)
* **Sets/組數**: 3–4
* **Reps/次數**: 10–12
* **Target/目標**: Long head (posterior tricep), heavily loaded in the stretched position. (長頭，在伸展位置進行高負荷訓練。)
